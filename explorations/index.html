Machine Learning in Football

The football world, explored.

A growing set of small, self-contained data explorations built from 150 years of international results — each one a single question, taken as far as the data honestly goes. Start anywhere.

The shape of the football world

02Forecast Live

Every match, predicted — then marked

A Dixon-Coles goals model rates every World Cup match as probabilities, frozen the day before kickoff — then grades itself against the actual results. A second tab opens the engine right up: the team ratings map and a live, step-by-step anatomy of one prediction.

Three host countries, sixteen venues, brutal distances. A from-scratch index of every team's kilometres, time-zones, altitude and rest — drag the weights and watch the ranking re-sort live.

04Club & Country Live

The league dependency map

Where all 1,246 squad players earn their living — a Sankey of nations into leagues, a domestic-dependency stat per team, and the unglamorous entity-resolution that makes it trustworthy.

05Player Similarity Live

Who plays like whom?

A per-90 fingerprint for every coverable squad player, embedded by position into a map you can explore. Pick a star and meet their five closest stylistic matches — similarity measured in full feature space, not on the map.

06Penalty Geometry Live

A coin flip that isn't

Shooter picks a side, keeper picks a side — every World Cup shootout kick as a 3×3 payoff matrix, with the keeper's actual dive on each one. A mixed-strategy equilibrium test, the abandoned centre, and a playable keeper game.

07Model vs Market Soon

Can a hobbyist model beat the closing line?

A Monte-Carlo model's probability is frozen before each kickoff and scored against the bookmakers' de-vigged odds — Brier, calibration, and the honest gap. Losing to the market is the expected, publishable result. Live once odds capture is running.
