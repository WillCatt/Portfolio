Machine Learning in Football · 06

A coin flip that isn't

A penalty looks like luck and feels like nerve. Read it as a game and it becomes a 3×3 grid: the shooter picks a side, the keeper picks a side, and a goal or a save falls out of whether they match. This time the data records which way the keeper actually dives — so we can finally play out the whole game, not just the shooter's half of it. Five acts on World Cup shootouts, then take the gloves yourself.

Every World Cup shootout kick, 1982–2022

Each dot is one kick, dropped into the part of the goal it was struck — saved or scored. The corners glow, the middle is sparse. Hold that picture: the rest of the piece is about what the keeper does while the ball is on its way.

Is anyone playing Nash?

If shooters are at equilibrium, every side they use should score equally

Game theory's one clean prediction: at a mixed-strategy equilibrium, every option you play must pay the same — otherwise you'd shift toward the better one. So do left, centre and right convert equally? (Chiappori, Levitt & Groseclose 2002; Palacios-Huerta 2003.)
