APPLICATION NO. 41872/19 KOWALSKI v. MERIDIAN CAPITAL PARTNERS LLP AND OTHERS JUDGMENT (Strasbourg, 12 March 2022) In the case of Kowalski v. Meridian Capital Partners LLP and Others, the European Court of Human Rights, sitting as a Chamber composed of Mr. H. Beecham, President, Ms E. Whitcombe, Vice-President, Mr. T. Ferreira, Judge, Ms A. Petrova, Judge, Mr. J. Carrow, Judge ad hoc, and Mr. R. Patel, Registrar, having deliberated in private on 3 and 10 February 2022, delivers the following judgment: FACTS I. THE CIRCUMSTANCES OF THE CASE 1. The applicant, Maria Anya Kowalski, born 15 April 1975, residing at 22 Malinova Street, Plovdiv 4000, Bulgaria, is a Bulgarian national of Roma ethnicity. At the relevant time she was employed as a registered nurse at the Sofia Regional Hospital Trust (hereinafter "the Trust") under employment contract ref. HR-2010-0042, a position she had held since January 2010. 2. On 4 February 2021, the applicant lodged a formal grievance (Internal Ref. HR-2021-0847) with the Trust's Board of Directors, chaired by Mr. Harold Beecham of 14 Carrow Road, Norwich NR1 1EE, United Kingdom. The applicant alleged unlawful discrimination on grounds of her Roma ethnicity and national origin, contrary to the Equality Act 2010, ss. 4, 9 and 13. 3. The applicant was represented in these proceedings by Patel & Rosenthal LLP (Company Registration No. 03241187, registered at 88 Wood Street, London EC2V 7RS), a firm specialising in employment and human rights law, and from 1 March 2021 additionally by Carrow Chambers (Bar Standards Board authorisation no. 51947). The Trust instructed St. Andrew's Regional Solicitors, Manchester. 4. On 12 June 2019 and again on 3 November 2020 the applicant reported incidents of discriminatory treatment to HR Director Ms Eleanor Whitcombe (employee ID EMP-0091). Neither report produced a formal investigation. Incident references: INC-2019-0612 and INC-2020-1103. 5. Tomás Ferreira, born 22 September 1968, general counsel of Halcyon Biotech GmbH (Handelsregister Frankfurt, HRB 109842; registered at Bahnhofstrasse 14, 60329 Frankfurt am Main), initiated parallel settlement proceedings on behalf of the respondent. He proposed that payment of EUR 4,200,000 (four million two hundred thousand euros) be wired to IBAN PT50 0002 0123 1234 5678 9015 4, the account held at Banco Nacional de Lisboa in the name of the applicant's legal representatives. 6. The matter was referred to the Employment Tribunal in Manchester (Case no. 2402319/2021) after internal mediation at St. Andrew's Regional Hospital Trust failed on 19 August 2021. The Tribunal noted that Ms Kowalski had submitted corroborating evidence from three colleagues — Dr. A. Nowak (GMC ref. 7012345), Sr. P. Dimitrov (NMC pin 12A3456E) and Mr. K. Osei (employee ID EMP-0234) — each of whom had witnessed at least one of the relevant incidents. II. SUBMISSIONS OF THE PARTIES 7. The Government submitted that the domestic proceedings had been fair and that the applicant had not been subjected to any discriminatory treatment contrary to Article 14. They relied on Directive 2000/43/EC and contended that national courts had applied the relevant legal standards correctly. 8. The applicant maintained that the Trust's internal investigation had been inadequate. She pointed to the fact that Mr. Beecham had previously sat on the board of a firm in which Mr. Ferreira held a directorship, raising a potential conflict of interest undisclosed at the time of the mediation. III. THE LAW 9. The Court reiterates that Article 14 does not have independent existence; it applies only in conjunction with the substantive Convention rights. In the present case the Court considers it necessary to examine the complaint under Article 14 taken together with Article 8. 10. Having regard to all the material before it, the Court finds that the difference in treatment of which the applicant complains was not justified by any objective and reasonable ground, and that the respondent State has therefore failed in its positive obligation under Article 14 read with Article 8 of the Convention. FOR THESE REASONS, THE COURT UNANIMOUSLY 1. Declares the application admissible; 2. Holds that there has been a violation of Article 14 of the Convention taken in conjunction with Article 8; 3. Holds that the respondent State is to pay the applicant, within three months from the date on which the judgment becomes final, EUR 24,500 (twenty-four thousand five hundred euros) in respect of non-pecuniary damage, plus any tax that may be chargeable. Done in English, and notified in writing on 12 March 2022, pursuant to Rule 77 §§ 2 and 3 of the Rules of Court. R. Patel H. Beecham Registrar President
